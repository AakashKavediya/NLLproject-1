The LARC (Lamakaan Amateur Radio Convention) that took place in Hyderabad in 2017 was a notable occasion designed to promote ingenuity and technical advancement among amateur radio enthusiasts. The convention included a variety of captivating sessions such as antenna construction competitions, technical workshops, and keynote speeches delivered by distinguished hams, making it a standout event in the Indian amateur radio scene. Two representatives from the Crown Club, Akshay Jain and Jatin Bhosale, took part in the Antenna Making Contest, where they crafted a Moxon Yagi antenna for the VHF 145 MHz band. The antenna was assessed and tested by Ashhar Farhan (VU2ESE), a prominent figure in the amateur radio community recognized for his innovations such as the sBitx transceiver. The antenna contest is a recurring event at LARC, inspiring participants to demonstrate their engineering skills in RF design.


The event also features:

  • workshops focused on SDR, antenna tuning, and transceiver engineering

  • technical presentations from industry experts

  • interactive sessions aimed at helping novice hams enhance their practical knowledge


LARC 2017 offered Crown Club members essential exposure to antenna theory, hands-on RF construction, and feedback from seasoned operators, marking an important milestone in their journey through amateur radio.
